Batchim

We call last consonant a 'Batchim'.
It's the last consonant that is placed in the under part of the Hangul.

When I teach Korean, I found a lot of students feel that Batchim is difficult.
They usually studied Batchim to sound like
'ㄱ' = [k], 'ㄴ' = [n]...
And they think the sound of the consonant changes when they are a Batchim.

I want to say the Batchim is not a sound but the movement of our articulator, like the mouth, tongue, throat etc...
Also, we have already learned about Batchim when we learned about consonants.
Isn't it cool? Let's check it out.
